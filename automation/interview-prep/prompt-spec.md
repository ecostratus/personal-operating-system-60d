# Interview Preparation Prompt Specification

## Purpose

Define the prompt structure for generating comprehensive interview preparation materials.

## Objectives

- Research company and role thoroughly
- Prepare relevant examples and stories
- Anticipate likely interview questions
- Develop strong questions to ask
- Build confidence through preparation

## Preparation Types

### 1. Company Research Brief
**Focus**: Company background, culture, recent news
**Output**: Research summary document

### 2. Role Analysis
**Focus**: Job requirements, team structure, expectations
**Output**: Role breakdown with alignment points

### 3. Question Preparation
**Focus**: Anticipated questions with prepared answers
**Output**: Q&A preparation guide

### 4. STAR Stories
**Focus**: Structured examples demonstrating skills
**Output**: Story bank with STAR format

### 5. Questions to Ask
**Focus**: Intelligent questions showing research and interest
**Output**: Tiered question list

## Prompt Structure

### Input Requirements
1. **Company Information**: Name, industry, size, recent news
2. **Job Posting**: Full description and requirements
3. **Your Background**: Resume and relevant experience
4. **Interview Stage**: Phone screen, technical, behavioral, final
5. **Interview Format**: One-on-one, panel, presentation, etc.

### Output Requirements
1. **Company Brief**: Key facts and talking points
2. **Role Alignment**: How you match requirements
3. **Anticipated Questions**: Likely questions with answer frameworks
4. **STAR Stories**: Prepared examples with details
5. **Questions to Ask**: Thoughtful questions by category

## Prompt Template

```
You are helping prepare for a job interview.

Company Information:
- Name: [Company name]
- Industry: [Industry]
- Size: [Employee count]
- Recent News: [Relevant developments]

Job Posting:
[Full job description]

Your Background:
[Resume or relevant experience]

Interview Details:
- Stage: [Phone screen / Technical / Behavioral / Final]
- Format: [One-on-one / Panel / Presentation]
- Interviewer(s): [Names/roles if known]

Instructions:
1. Research and summarize key company information
2. Analyze role requirements and alignment
3. Generate likely interview questions
4. Suggest relevant STAR stories from background
5. Develop thoughtful questions to ask
6. Provide confidence-building talking points

Provide:
- Company research brief
- Role alignment analysis
- Anticipated questions (10-15)
- STAR story suggestions (5-7)
- Questions to ask (tiered by priority)
- Key talking points
```

## Company Research

### Research Areas
- Mission, vision, values
- Products/services
- Recent news and developments
- Funding/financial status
- Competitors and market position
- Culture indicators
- Leadership team

### Research Sources
- Company website and blog
- LinkedIn company page
- Recent press releases
- Glassdoor reviews
- Industry news
- Social media presence

### Output Format
- Executive summary (bullet points)
- Key talking points
- Red flags or concerns
- Excitement factors

## Question Preparation

### Common Question Categories

#### Background Questions
- "Tell me about yourself"
- "Walk me through your resume"
- "Why are you looking for a new role?"

#### Behavioral Questions
- "Tell me about a time when..."
- "Describe a situation where..."
- "Give me an example of..."

#### Technical Questions
- Role-specific technical skills
- Problem-solving scenarios
- Tool/technology experience

#### Situational Questions
- "What would you do if..."
- "How would you handle..."
- "How would you approach..."

#### Motivation Questions
- "Why this company?"
- "Why this role?"
- "What are your career goals?"

### Answer Framework

For each question:
1. **Question**: Exact wording of likely question
2. **Key Points**: Main points to cover
3. **Example**: Specific story or example if applicable
4. **Pitfalls**: What to avoid in answer

## STAR Story Development

### STAR Format
- **Situation**: Set the context
- **Task**: Describe the challenge or goal
- **Action**: Explain what you did
- **Result**: Share the outcome with metrics

### Story Bank Categories
- Leadership/influence
- Problem-solving
- Teamwork/collaboration
- Conflict resolution
- Innovation/creativity
- Handling failure
- Technical achievement

### Quality Standards
- Specific, not generic
- Include metrics when possible
- Show your role clearly
- End with positive result
- Keep to 2-3 minutes when told

## Questions to Ask

### Question Tiers

#### Tier 1: Role-Specific (Always Ask)
- Day-to-day responsibilities
- Success metrics
- Team structure
- Immediate priorities

#### Tier 2: Team/Company (Ask Most)
- Team culture and dynamics
- Company challenges and opportunities
- Growth and development
- Why interviewer joined/stays

#### Tier 3: Deep/Strategic (Ask If Time)
- Strategic direction
- Industry trends
- Long-term vision
- Innovation approach

### Question Quality
- Show you've done research
- Demonstrate interest in contribution
- Reveal values alignment
- Avoid easily googleable answers
- Don't ask about salary/benefits too early

## Review Process

### Human Review Checklist
- [ ] Research is current and accurate
- [ ] Questions are relevant to role
- [ ] STAR stories are specific and strong
- [ ] Questions to ask show genuine interest
- [ ] Preparation builds confidence
- [ ] No overpreparation/rigidity

## Practice Recommendations

### Before Interview
- Review company brief
- Practice STAR stories aloud
- Rehearse answer frameworks (don't memorize)
- Prepare questions to ask
- Practice with friend/mock interview

### Mental Preparation
- Visualize successful interview
- Prepare mindset: curious, confident, authentic
- Remember: You're evaluating them too
- Plan logistics (what to wear, how to get there)
